# Inspiration Library

> A personal collection of lyrics, poems, prose, or any other lines that resonate. The
> skill reads this at session start to calibrate stylistic and thematic instincts when
> drafting. The point isn't to imitate or quote these directly — it's to give the skill
> a sense of the moves, registers, and emotional textures that land for you.

## How to add an entry

Copy the entry block below, paste it under `## Entries`, and fill it in. `Source`,
`Excerpt`, and `What resonates` are the important fields. The rest is optional.

The `What resonates` field matters most — that's what calibrates the skill on your
taste. Be specific. "The way the second line undercuts the first" is more useful than
"good imagery." Plain language is fine.

```markdown
### [Short label or memorable first line]
- **Source:** Title — Artist or Author (Year, Album/Collection if useful)
- **Type:** lyric | poem | prose | other
- **Excerpt:**
  > the actual lines, quoted

  > additional stanza if needed
- **What resonates:** Free-form prose on why this lands — the technique, the angle, the
  move it makes, the feeling, the register. Specifics teach the skill more than
  adjectives.
- **Tags:** comma-separated, free-form. Themes, techniques, moods. Examples:
  domestic-detail, understatement, turn, second-person, religious-imagery,
  plain-spoken.
- **Added:** YYYY-MM-DD
```
