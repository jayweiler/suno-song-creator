# Suno v4.5 / v4.5+ / v4.5-All

> **Released:** v4.5 in May 2025, v4.5+ in July 2025. v4.5-All replaced v3.5 on
> the free tier in 2025.
>
> Last generation before the v5 NLP shift. Comma-tag prompts work reliably here;
> conversational prompts started working but are less consistent than on v5+.
> Best when the user wants predictable behavior, fast iteration, or one of the
> Pro-only v4.5+ workflows (Add Vocals, Add Instrumentals, Spark from Playlist).

## Variants

- **v4.5** (May 2025) — base model. 8-min first-gen length. Smarter mashups
  than v4 (e.g., midwest emo + neosoul, EDM + folk).
- **v4.5+** (July 2025) — adds Add Vocals, Add Instrumentals, Spark from
  Playlist. Pro/Premier only.
- **v4.5-All** — v4.5 variant available on the free tier (replaced v3.5 free
  access in 2025). Faster, more expressive than v3.5.

For prompting purposes, all three behave identically. Differences are in
features, not generation.

## Prompt format

**Hybrid — comma-tag works reliably; conversational started working but is less
consistent than on v5.**

- ✅ Comma-tag: "deep house, emotional, melodic, hypnotic rhythms, organic textures"
- ✅ Conversational (works, but less reliably than v5): "Create a melodic,
  emotional deep house song with organic textures and hypnotic rhythms"
- ⚠️ Heavy narrative prose ("a melancholic deep house track that feels like
  driving at 2am when the city is empty") — may produce flatter results than
  the same prose on v5 because v4.5's NLP layer is weaker.

**Recommendation when shaping prompts for v4.5:** lead with comma-tag style
for the genre/mood/instrument descriptors, then add a single short sentence
for the emotional or narrative direction if needed. Don't write the whole
prompt as flowing prose — v5+ will reward that, v4.5 will under-honor it.

Tokenizer note: the first ~3 words carry more weight than the last ten.
Front-load priority — genre/subgenre first.

## Style char limit & truncation

- **1000 characters.** Same as v5 and v5.5.
- Front-load. If truncated, the tail should be the least important content.

## Cue reliability — what to use

Section tags and inline parenthetical cues exist on v4.5 but are **less
reliable than on v5+, especially across later sections**. v4.5 honors a tag in
its immediate section but often "forgets" the instruction by the time the song
returns to a similar section later.

Practical implications:

- **One-shot tags (single section):** `(whispered)`, `(spoken)`, `(belted)` on
  a single line work reasonably.
- **Tags meant to persist (whole song):** unreliable. If the user wants the
  whole song spoken or whispered, prefer a strong style-prompt directive AND
  the section tag, so the prompt reinforces the cue.
- **Bracketed performance modifiers** (`[Whispered Verse]`) — usable but
  expect drift. Reinforce in the style prompt.
- **Mood cues** (`(haunting, intimate)`, `(playful)`) — least reliable on
  v4.5. Often dropped.

If the song is structurally complex with many cues, suggest v5 or v5.5 instead
— they hold cues across callbacks reliably.

## v4.5+ Pro-only features

These are unique to v4.5+ and not in v4.5 base or v4.5-All:

- **Add Vocals** — turn an instrumental idea into a full song by layering on
  vocals based on lyrics. Workflow: upload an instrumental, generate one with
  the Instrumental toggle, or use stems from an existing song; then Add Vocals
  generates a vocal layer for the lyrics.
- **Add Instrumentals** — start from a vocal and build a backing track. Upload
  vocals or hum a melody; v4.5+ generates a high-quality instrumental.
- **Spark from Playlist** — seed a new generation from a playlist (older,
  less-constrained predecessor of v5.5's Inspire feature).

## Other v4.5 / v4.5+ features

- 8-min first-generation length (v4.5+ specifically; v4.5 base was 4-min one-shot
  with Extend up to 2 min per call).
- Cover, Extend, Mashup, Persona (the older Persona workflow before the v5.5
  rename to Voices).
- Use as Inspiration / Inspo tab — works here too, with broader source tolerance
  than v5.5's narrowed Inspire flow.

## When to choose v4.5 / v4.5+

- The user wants predictable comma-tag behavior. v4.5 is the most "do what I
  said" model in this group.
- The user is iterating fast and wants quick generation.
- The user is on the free tier (use v4.5-All).
- The user needs Add Vocals or Add Instrumentals (v4.5+ Pro).
- The song is short, simple, and structurally regular — cue-reliability gaps
  don't matter much.

## When to choose v5 / v5.5 instead

- The song needs reliable cue control across many sections (use v5+).
- The user wants narrative-prompt understanding (e.g., "warm upright bass enters
  in verse two and holds long tones from there"). v4.5 will under-honor this;
  v5+ will time the arrangement from the prose.
- The user wants the audio quality of v5+'s vocal naturalness, low-end
  separation, and stereo imaging.
- The user wants v5.5-only features (Voices, Custom Models, Replace Section).
