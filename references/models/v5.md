# Suno v5

> **Released:** September 2025 | **Status:** Active. Pro/Premier.
>
> The model where Suno's prompt understanding shifted from keyword-tag matching to
> NLP-driven narrative interpretation. Strong default for songs that need raw,
> unpolished, or austere texture — v5.5 over-polishes those aesthetics.

## Headline differences from v4.5

- **NLP-driven prompt understanding.** v5 added genuine narrative comprehension.
  Sentences like "a melancholic deep house track that feels like driving at 2am
  when the city is empty" work — the model interprets the emotional imagery, not
  just the genre label. v4.5 still leaned on keyword-tag matching.
- **Better adherence to style prompts.** "lo-fi hip hop with rhodes piano" actually
  sounds like that on v5, rather than a generic approximation.
- **Cue tags reliable across the song.** v4.5 honored meta tags in the immediate
  section but often dropped them on later callbacks. v5 holds tag instructions
  through the full song.
- **Studio mode** — separate stems (vocals, bass, drums, harmony, instrumentation)
  on independent tracks for DAW-style editing.
- **Audio quality:** noticeably better vocal naturalness (less "AI singer"),
  cleaner low-frequency separation (bass and kick), and stronger stereo imaging
  than v4.5.

## Headline differences from v5.5

- **More texture, less polish.** v5 keeps more grit. v5.5 sands edges off and
  delivers a stem-friendly, slightly quieter master. For songs that should sound
  raw or austere, v5 is the right pick.
- **No Voices.** v5 cannot use the user's biometric voice upload — that's v5.5
  only.
- **No Custom Models / My Taste / Replace Section.** All v5.5 additions.
- **Louder, more radio-ready** master out of the box (v5.5 delivers ~1.5–2 dB
  quieter integrated loudness with more headroom).

## Prompt format

**Narrative / NLP-driven.** v5 was the format pivot from v4.5's keyword lists.

- ✅ "Begin with soft ambient layers, natural sounds, and a deep steady groove.
  Build gradually with flowing melodic synths and warm basslines."
- ❌ "ambient layers, deep groove, melodic synths, warm bass" (works, but flattens
  arrangement timing into "all present from the start")

The narrative form lets the model time arrangement changes (when instruments
enter, when dynamics shift). Comma-tag lists work in a pinch but lose
specificity at scale.

Tokenizer note: the first ~3 words of the style field carry more weight than
the last ten. Front-load priority — genre/subgenre first, then vocal direction,
then mood, then instruments, then BPM, then production notes.

## Style char limit & truncation

- **1000 characters.**
- A well-crafted 400–600 char prompt usually outperforms a cluttered 900+ char one.
- Front-load priority. If the prompt is going to lose its tail, the tail should
  be the least important content.

## Cue reliability — what to use

Cues that work on v5.5 generally work on v5. Reliability is high for vocal
delivery cues, medium-high for dynamics cues, medium for mood cues. The
vocabulary tables in `models/v5-5.md` apply here too.

Notable:
- Bracketed section-level performance modifiers (`[Whispered Verse]`,
  `[Spoken Bridge]`, etc.) work reliably.
- Inline parenthetical cues (`(whispered)`, `(spoken)`, `(belted)`) work
  reliably and are honored across callbacks (a v4.5 weakness v5 fixed).
- One cue per section. Stacking multiple cues confuses the model.

### Failure modes

- Suno can ignore cues if surrounding context strongly suggests otherwise.
- First generation may not honor a cue; second often does. Re-roll once before
  assuming the cue is broken.
- A cue that lands once may not land identically on regeneration — Suno is
  stochastic.

## v5-specific UI & features

- **Studio mode** — multitrack stem editing. Vocals, bass, drums, harmony,
  instrumentation each on a separate track. Use for fine post-generation mixing
  or extracting specific elements.
- **Standard Suno UI features** — Cover, Extend, Mashup, Reuse Prompt, Crop,
  Use as Inspiration / Inspo tab (up to 4 reference tracks).
- **No Voices, no Custom Models, no My Taste, no Replace Section.** Those are
  all v5.5-only.

## When to choose v5

- The song is meant to feel raw, lo-fi, austere, dark ambient, raw black metal,
  or otherwise unpolished. v5.5 will sand the edges off; v5 won't.
- The user wants the loudest, most radio-ready master out of the box.
- The user doesn't need any v5.5-only feature.
- The user wants reliable narrative-prompt understanding without the v5.5 audio
  profile shift.

## When to choose v5.5 instead

- The user wants polished, professional output for radio/streaming-friendly
  genres (pop, EDM, mainstream rock).
- The user is using or training Voices / Custom Models.
- The user needs Replace Section's surgical regeneration.
- The user will mix in a DAW and wants more headroom.
